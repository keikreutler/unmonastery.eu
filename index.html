The unMonastery aims to develop a new kind of social space, akin to co-living and co-working spaces, drawing influence from both Monasteries and HackerSpaces.

    With a focus on the process of co-creation and co-learning between the community and unMonasterians, the unMonastery addresses the interlinked needs of empty space, unemployment and depleting social services.

    “When it comes to work it is increasingly difficult to reconcile making money with making sense. People do work to make a living. Others do work to make meaning. But the two works are not the same work.”

    This place-based social innovation is an ambitious and radical response to the challenge of bridging this gap. It draws inspiration from the 10th century monastic life to encourage radical forms of social innovation and collaboration. A sort of lay, off-grid mendicant order striving for a society that can better withstand present and future systemic crises through building resilient, networked communities.
